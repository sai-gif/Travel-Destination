Culture and History

Destination Culture History Traditions
Glacier National Park The culture of the local tribes, including the Blackfeet Nation and Salish-Kootenai people, plays a significant role in the park's heritage. Established in 1910, the park has a rich history of exploration and conservation efforts. Annual cultural events and ceremonies are held by the local tribes, showcasing their traditions and heritage.
Maui Maui is a diverse destination with a blend of Native Hawaiian, Asian, and Western cultures. It has a storied history as a center for the whaling industry and later as a hub for tourism. Traditional hula, luaus, and cultural festivals celebrate the island's heritage.
Grand Canyon National Park The park is rich in Native American culture, with the Havasupai, Hualapai, Navajo, and Hopi tribes having historical ties to the canyon. First explored by Europeans in the 16th century, the Grand Canyon has a deep history of scientific discovery and exploration. Native American ceremonies and dances are often performed within the park, connecting visitors with indigenous traditions.
